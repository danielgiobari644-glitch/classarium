Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us when you create an account, configure your school profile, or communicate with us. This includes your name, email address, phone number, school name, and other contact or identification details. For staff and student accounts created through the platform, we collect the information that school administrators enter, such as names, admission numbers, class assignments, and academic records.

Automatically collected information includes your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URLs, and interaction patterns within the Service. We also collect log data when you use our API or CLI tools, including request timestamps, endpoints accessed, and response status codes.

We may also collect information from third-party services you choose to integrate with Classarium, such as Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or payment processors. The scope of data shared by these integrations is governed by the permissions you grant and the respective third-party privacy policies.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to provide, maintain, and improve the Classarium Service, including processing academic records, generating report cards, managing attendance, and facilitating school administration. Your data enables us to deliver personalized features such as dashboard analytics, student performance tracking, and automated notifications.

We also use collected information for account security and fraud prevention purposes, such as detecting unauthorized access attempts, verifying your identity during password resets, and monitoring for suspicious activity. We may use aggregated, anonymized data for internal analytics, product development, and improving our machine learning models that power features like grade predictions and attendance pattern analysis.

With your consent, we may send you service-related communications, including product updates, security alerts, and administrative notifications. You can manage your communication preferences in your account settings. We will never sell your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Data Storage & Security

All data is stored on secure, enterprise-grade cloud infrastructure hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS) with data centers in the United States and the European Union. We employ encryption at rest using AES-256 and encryption in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher for all data transmissions. Database backups are performed continuously and stored in geographically separate locations to ensure data durability and disaster recovery.

Our security infrastructure includes multi-factor authentication for internal systems, role-based access controls, automated vulnerability scanning, regular penetration testing by independent third parties, and a dedicated security operations team monitoring for threats 24/7. We maintain SOC 2 Type II compliance and undergo annual security audits.

Despite our best efforts, no electronic system is completely secure. We encourage you to use strong, unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication on your account, and promptly report any suspected security incidents to our support team. In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify affected users within 72 hours as required by applicable data protection laws.

4. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We share data only with service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as cloud hosting (AWS), payment processing (Stripe), email delivery (SendGrid), and analytics. These providers are contractually obligated to process data only as instructed by us and to maintain appropriate security measures.

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, in response to valid legal process such as a subpoena, court order, or government request, or when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others, investigate fraud, or respond to a government request.

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, user data may be transferred to the acquiring entity. We will notify you via email and/or a prominent notice on our website before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

5. Cookies & Tracking

Classarium uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze usage patterns, and deliver personalized content. Essential cookies are necessary for the Service to function properly, including session management, authentication tokens, and security features. These cannot be disabled without impacting core functionality.

Analytics cookies, including those from Google Analytics, help us understand how users interact with the Service by collecting information about pages visited, time on site, and navigation paths. Marketing cookies from platforms like LinkedIn and Google Ads may be used to deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign effectiveness. You can manage your cookie preferences through the cookie consent banner or your browser settings.

We do not use third-party cookies for cross-site tracking on our application pages. Our tracking is limited to our own domains and services. We respect Do Not Track (DNT) signals sent by your browser and will not override your preference where technically feasible.

6. User Rights & Access

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data, including the right to access, correct, delete, or port your data. You can exercise these rights by contacting our Data Protection Officer at privacy@classarium.com or through the privacy settings in your account dashboard. We will respond to all requests within 30 days, or as required by applicable law.

School administrators have the ability to export all school-related data, including student records, academic results, and attendance history, in standard machine-readable formats (CSV, JSON, or PDF) at any time. Upon account termination, administrators can request a complete data export, and we will provide it within 14 business days.

If you are a student or parent and wish to access, correct, or delete personal data, please contact your school's Classarium administrator first. If your request cannot be resolved through the school, you may contact us directly. We will verify your identity before processing any data-related request.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. Upon account termination, we will retain certain data for a limited period as required by law (typically 3–7 years for financial and academic records), for legitimate business purposes such as resolving disputes, and to comply with educational record-keeping regulations.

Academic records, including grades, attendance data, and report cards, are retained for the duration specified by the school's retention policy or as required by applicable education laws. This data is preserved even if a school's subscription ends, to ensure students' academic histories are not lost. Schools can request permanent deletion of their data at any time, subject to legal retention requirements.

Automatically collected usage data and logs are retained for a maximum of 24 months unless a longer retention period is required for security investigation or legal compliance. Marketing and analytics data linked to individual users is retained for 13 months from the last interaction, after which it is anonymized.

8. Children's Privacy

Classarium is designed for use by educational institutions and their authorized personnel. Student data is entered and managed by school administrators, teachers, and staff who are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable child privacy laws, including the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) in the United States and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the European Union for minors.

We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under the age of 13. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13 without verified parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information as quickly as possible. School administrators are responsible for obtaining any required parental consents for the collection and processing of student data.

Our platform includes features that help schools comply with student privacy requirements, including access controls, audit logging, and data export capabilities. We encourage all schools to establish clear data governance policies and to review our data processing agreement (DPA) available upon request.

9. International Transfers

Classarium is a global service, and your data may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own. Our primary data centers are located in the United States (us-east-1, us-west-2) and the European Union (eu-west-1). When data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission to ensure adequate protection.

For schools operating in specific regions with data localization requirements, we offer region-locked deployment options that ensure all data processing occurs within the specified jurisdiction. Please contact our sales team for information about regional data residency options and associated pricing.

We comply with applicable data transfer frameworks, including the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework, UK Extension, and Swiss-U.S. Data Privacy Framework. Our Data Processing Agreement, available upon request, details the specific safeguards and mechanisms we employ for international data transfers.

10. Changes to Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of material changes by posting a prominent notice on our website, sending an email to the address associated with your account, or through an in-app notification at least 30 days before the changes take effect.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the most recent revision was made. Your continued use of the Service after any changes become effective constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

An archive of previous versions of this Privacy Policy is maintained and available upon request. If you have questions about a specific change, please contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@classarium.com.
